# Sikizana Brand Guide

The rules that keep every surface — pages, prompts, emails, error
messages — telling one story. When writing ANY user-facing copy, check
it against this file.

## The one-line story

**Sikizana gets small businesses paid.** Tagline everywhere:
`Get paid faster · Works with Xero`.

## The duo rule (the core principle)

Sikizana is two owls with one job — your money:

| | **Siki** | **Zana** |
|---|---|---|
| Role | The Explainer | The Enforcer |
| Owns | Trust, watching, explaining | Action, chasing, enforcement |
| Voice | Gentle, patient, allergic to jargon, reassuring | Direct, unbothered, urgency without panic, never cruel |
| Accent | Sky/orange | Rose/dark stone |
| Example line | "You're owed £4,200. Here's who, and for how long. Want me to help?" | "It's been 45 days. Here's the email to send. You're also owed £70 in compensation — I added it." |

**Each owl appears wherever *their* job is described.** Consent screens,
security/privacy promises, audits, digests, explanations → Siki. Chase
sequences, reminder approvals, escalation, hard truths → Zana. The
pricing tiers follow it too: Free = Siki watches, Pro = Zana acts,
Business = both.

Getting this wrong reads as a bug: "Let Siki chase what you're owed"
shipped once and credited the wrong owl.

### Where the owls must NOT appear

- **Chase emails and the Letter Before Action.** Those send under the
  USER'S business name to their customers. A mascot in a statutory
  notice undermines the letter and the user. Debtors never see the owls.
- Anywhere it would soften a legal or financial fact into a joke.

## Honesty rules (the brand IS honesty)

1. **Every claim must trace to something real.** No email addresses,
   domains, certifications, response-time promises, or company details
   that don't verifiably exist. (hello@sikizana.com shipped once — we
   don't own that domain. Contact is hello@persidian.com.)
2. **Demo vs. live is always labelled.** Sample data never masquerades
   as the user's numbers — including in marketing surfaces (the pricing
   page only shows YOUR overdue total in live mode).
3. **Benchmarks are "typical UK ranges · indicative"** — never "ONS
   data" or live statistics. Guesses (like sector detection) are
   disclosed as guesses.
4. **Writes are approval-gated and we say so constantly**: "Siki can't
   change anything, Zana can't chase anyone — without you."
5. **No dark patterns.** No countdown urgency, exit-intent discounts,
   hard paywalls, or review-begging. Personality rides on real product
   moments (money recovered, invoice paid), never on manufactured ones.

## Voice mechanics

- Plain English first; when an accounting term is unavoidable, gloss it
  in one sentence ("Unreconciled = a bank transaction Xero can't match
  yet").
- Money numbers lead. "£4,200 slipping away" beats "3 overdue invoices".
- Loss-aversion framing for problems, celebration for wins — the full
  celebration is reserved for the payment moment (a chased invoice got
  PAID), which is the product's climax.
- Speech-bubble Siki intros for trust/policy pages (see /security,
  /privacy for the pattern).

## Canonical copy

- Nav tagline: `Get paid faster · Works with Xero`
- CTA to the app: `Open Sikizana` (never "Open Bookkeeper")
- Chat footer: `Sikizana · Get paid faster · Human-in-the-loop by design`
- The duo promise: `Siki can't change anything, Zana can't chase anyone —
  without you.`
- Contact: `hello@persidian.com` (we do NOT own sikizana.com)
